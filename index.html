About the Russian Tail

The "Russian Tail" refers to an anomaly in election data where the combined distribution shows a telltale pattern of two overlapping bell curves - one representing genuine votes and another representing potentially fraudulent votes.

In this playground, you can adjust the parameters of both distributions to see how they appear when combined. By default, the fake votes are positioned to the right of the real votes (at μ=11 vs μ=8), with 1/4 the amplitude.
